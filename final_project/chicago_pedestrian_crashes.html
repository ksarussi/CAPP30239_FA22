Hundreds of Pedestrians are Hit by Vehicles Every Month

There are some data quality issues in the early months of 2017. It is probable that the number of pedestrains hit by vehicles is underreported in the data prior to the steep pickup in AUGUST 2017??

The number of pedestrians hit by vehicles each month dropped as the COVID-19 pandemic picked up in March 2020. The lowest number of crashes in one month occurred in April 2020, in the midst of the COVID-19 pandemic. Since then, number of pedestrians hit by vehicles each month have trended towards the pre-pandemic average.

Source: Chicago Open Data Portal

Most Pedestrians' Injuries are Recorded as Non-Incapacitating

"Reported, Non-evident" injuries are any injuries not included in the other categories or that are not visible/evident. Non-incapacitating injuries are defined as "any injury, other than a fatal or incapacitating injury, which is evident to observers at the scene of the crash. This includes lumps on the head, abrasions, bruises, minor lacerations."

Source: Chicago Open Data Portal
