Hundreds of Pedestrians are Hit by Vehicles Every Month

Source: "Traffic Crashes - People" and "Traffic Crashes - Vision Zero Traffic Fatalities"

The number of pedestrians hit by vehicles declined as the COVID-19 pandemic began in March 2020. The lowest number of crashes in one month occurred in April 2020. Since early 2021, the number of pedestrians hit has trended towards pre-pandemic averages.

Most Pedestrian's Injuries are Reported as Non-Incapacitating

Source: "Traffic Crashes - People", "Traffic Crashes - Vision Zero Traffic Fatalities"

and KABCO Injury Classification Definitions

"Reported, Non-evident" are any injuries not included in the other categories, or are injuries that are not visible/evident. This may include "momentary unconsciousness, claims of injuries not evident, limping, complaints of pain, nausea, hysteria"

"Non-incapacitating" refers to any injuries that are not classified as fatal or incapacitating and are evident at the scene of the crash. This may include "lumps on the head, abrasions, bruises, minor lacerations."

"Incapacitating" refers to any injuries "which prevents the injured person from walking, driving, or normally continuing the activities" that were possible before the crash occurred.

Pedestrian Fatalities in 2022

Each dot represents one fatality between 2017 and Oct. 2022

From January through October, thirty-eight pedestrians have been killed.

Source: Chicago Open Data Portal

The city of Chicago has a "Fatal Crash Response Coordination Committee" and uses a process to determine whether a death is a "traffic fatality" for the purposes of Vision Zero Chicago (an initiative created with the goal of zero traffic deaths in Chicago). I choose to display the number of pedestrian fatalities as defined by the FCRCC. This number may thus represent an underestimate of total pedestrian fatalities over time due to the narrow definition of "traffic fatality" by the FCRCC."
